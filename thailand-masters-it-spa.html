Scholarships for Masters Programs

Funding opportunities are available for international students pursuing Master's degrees in Thailand. Here's a look at scholarships offered by Walailak University as a prominent example.

Walailak University Graduate Scholarships 2025

Walailak University offers graduate scholarships for both Master's and Ph.D. degrees, including various IT and Computer Science related fields. These scholarships aim to attract academically capable international students and support advanced studies and research.

Benefits:

  • Full or partial tuition fee waivers
  • Monthly living allowances to cover everyday costs
  • Access to high-quality research facilities
  • Opportunities to collaborate with experienced professors
  • Chances to work as teaching or research assistants (TA/RA)

Eligibility for Master's Degree Applicants:

  • Bachelor's degree from a recognized institution.
  • Minimum GPA of 3.00 on a 4.00 scale (or equivalent). Applications with a lower GPA may be considered if the applicant has significant research published in Scopus-indexed journals.
  • Demonstrated English language proficiency (e.g., TOEFL score of 500 paper-based or equivalent).
  • Not a recipient of scholarships of the same type from other organizations.
  • Must study full-time at Walailak University.

Applications are accepted on a rolling basis. For full details and to apply, always refer to the official Walailak University Graduate Studies website.

Visit Walailak Scholarships

Other Prominent Thai Scholarships:

  • Thai Government Scholarships (TIPP): Offered by TICA, often fully funded.
  • Chulalongkorn University Scholarships: Various scholarships for international graduate students, often covering full tuition and living expenses.
  • Asian Institute of Technology (AIT) Scholarships: Range of scholarships, including Royal Thai Government Scholarships, often covering full tuition and living costs.
  • Mahidol University Scholarships: Various scholarships ranging from tuition fee waivers to full scholarships.

These are just a few examples. Many other Thai universities also offer their own scholarships. Thorough research on individual university websites is highly recommended.

Example Study Plan: Walailak University M.Sc. IT Programs

Master's programs at Walailak University (e.g., in Computer Science, Data Science & AI) typically offer different study plans emphasizing research or a combination of coursework and research. Here's a general overview based on common structures. ✨ Click "Clarify" for explanations!

Master of Science Program Structure (e.g., Data Science and AI)

The program generally requires a total of 36 credits, typically completed over 2 years (4 semesters). It usually offers two main types:

Type A1: Research-Only (36 Credits Thesis) Clarify ✨

  • Focus: Pure research, suitable for students aiming for a Ph.D. or research-intensive careers.
  • Core Components:
    • Seminar I & II (Non-credit)
    • Thesis (36 Credits)
  • Graduation Requirement: Publication of at least one research paper at an international standard (e.g., in a Scopus-indexed journal).

Type A2: Coursework and Research (36 Credits: e.g., 9 Credits Coursework + 27 Credits Thesis/Independent Study) Clarify ✨

  • Focus: Balances theoretical knowledge through coursework with practical research experience.
  • Core Components:
    • Seminar I & II (Non-credit)
    • Core Courses (e.g., 9-12 credits)
    • Thesis or Independent Study (Remaining credits)
  • Example Core/Elective Course Areas: Clarify ✨
    • Research Methodology
    • Statistical Data Analytics
    • Scientific Writing
    • Data Modeling and Management
    • Machine Learning
    • Artificial Intelligence: Natural Language Understanding
    • Advanced Topics in Data Science and AI
  • Graduation Requirement: Typically includes coursework completion and publication of at least one research paper (or equivalent) in an international academic journal.

This is a generalized structure. Specific course offerings and credit distribution may vary slightly by program and academic year. Always consult the official curriculum of your chosen program on the university website.

General Masters Admissions Process in Thailand

While specific requirements vary by university and program, here's a typical flow for applying to a Master's degree in Thailand. Always check the university's official graduate school website.

1. Research Programs & Universities
2. Prepare Required Documents
3. Submit Online Application
4. Attend Interview (if required)
5. Receive Offer & Enroll

Typical Required Documents:

  • Bachelor's Degree Certificate & Transcripts (official, translated)
  • English Proficiency Test Scores (TOEFL/IELTS, if applicable)
  • Statement of Purpose/Personal Statement
  • Two Letters of Recommendation
  • Resume/CV
  • Copy of Passport
  • Application Fee Payment Receipt

Always verify the exact document list on the university's graduate school website for your chosen program.
